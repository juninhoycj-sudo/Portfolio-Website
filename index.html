NTU Sports Matching

June 2026

Built for NTU SummerBuild 2026. A mobile app that helps NTU students find, host and join pickup sports games within seconds. Built using free tier tools like Supabase and test deployed on ExpoGo. The app features many social aspects and tools that promote continued long term use and adoption like: friend system, streaks, live maps, ratings and comments.

This was my second hackathon, atlhough a week apart from my first, it was a great learning experience. I reinforced my understanding that hackathons are not all about great ideas and execution but also about having a great presentation pitch, a compelling story and clear explanations. We built too much and tried to make it too polished and functional instead of building a more novel and unique solution, which in hindsight was the whole purpose of hackathons... Great ideas.

In future, I should be looking into spending more time on the ideation phase and crafting up an idea that is flexible and unique before diving into the build. We should have also maybe looked into using the AI model from the partnering company REKA AI, or even integrating AI in our build.

Although we didn't win any awards, we were proud of our project as it addressed a reccuring problem in our student lives.

VerifySG

June 2026

Built for DSTA Brainhack Code_EXP 2026. VerifySG is a webapp that assists users to detect scams in photos, screenshots, text and links. It was built using Node.js, and routed through Gemini API and their Gemini 3.5 Flash model to analyse the media, and return an in-depth analysis, red flags, steps to take. The app allows users to share a simple warning card to their friends and family via social media and messaging platforms as an image, and also post warnings to their community. This community dashboard can be seen by all users to view recent warnings and take precaution.

This was my first hackathon experience, we made it through qualifiers and placed top 25 as finalists, and although it isn't a traditional overnight hackathon, it was an extremely great experience. Our team was highly inexperienced and rusty, having not done much since graduating from polytechnic and we took this as an opportunity to refresh and learn. Aaaaand we realised we were out of our depth. HAHAHAHAHA.

There were a lot of learning points, first was coming up with a unique solution that addresses the topics set by the organisers. With very specific target demographics and issues, it was hard to come up with very novel ideas, and this led me to realise that I don't just need to have great technical ability, but also think more about problems and ideas on the daily. Having a more inquisitve outlook on my surroundings and jotting them down will be key to coming up with newer ideas in future.

Second was the role of AI in today's field. Agentic AI has leveled the field and is revolutionary. Builds that would take weeks or months can be completed in days. Everyone can build apps and websites, but the key is in the human aspects of your build. The UI design, user experience, the idea and problem it solves, these are all key things that AI cannot replicate. Sure it is easy to just tell Claude to make me an app, but there is also skill in prompt engineering, slowly building a website from scratch with a vision in mind, sets you apart from the hundreds and thousands of generated websites popping up everyday by students.

Lastly is the art of presentation. I can't just have a great idea, I need to sell it. Every team I saw and interacted with had a compelling story, great visuals and well rehearsed lines. It made me truly believe in their product and its impact. Whereas our team sort of winged the presentation, kinda just diving into the app and showing it to the judges. We didn't really see the importance of the presentation and it showed. The judges were disengaged and questioned the effectiveness of our solution and the target demographic it will serve. I think it will be beneficial to learn more from my peers in school about their presentation techniques and tools they use to draw interest.
